Common questions

Yes, it really is that simple.

Do I need to know anything about AI?
Nothing at all. No prompts, no sliders, no settings. Pick a photo, tap one of ten plainly worded actions, and wait about thirty seconds to a minute. The AI stays in the background. You never talk to it.
Will it still look like the person?
Yes. Mend Memories restores the photo without redrawing the face. No plastic skin, no smoothed-away features. The goal is the person you remember, not a lookalike.
Can it fix badly torn or water-damaged photos?
That's what Heavy Damage Repair is for. It rebuilds the missing parts of a photo instead of blurring over the damage, so torn corners, deep creases, and water stains come back as real detail.
What kinds of photos can it restore?
Printed photos of any age: Polaroids, studio portraits, school and military photos, wedding albums, faded color prints from the 70s and 80s, sepia tintypes, and photos that have been folded, torn, or water-damaged. If you can hold it and snap it, we can probably restore it.
What happens to my photos?
When you restore a photo, it's sent to the AI image model that does the work, and the result comes back to you. Your photo isn't used to train AI, and we don't store it on our end. Sign in with Apple means we never ask for your name or email.
What are the Pro effects?
Three premium effects — Studio, Headshot, and Golden Hour — that go past restoration into a finished, professional look. Unlock them once for $1.99 and they're yours forever on any plan.
Is it really iOS-only?
For now, yes. Mend Memories is built for iPhone and iPad. We're watching demand for Android. If you'd like one, tell us.
How big are the restored photos?
Every restoration comes back at 2K, ready to print. Most people frame the result at 8×10 or larger with no loss in quality.
What happens after the free restorations?
The free trial is three restorations, not a clock. Once you've used them, you decide whether to subscribe. Nothing is charged unless you pick a plan, and you can cancel anytime from Settings → Subscriptions on your iPhone.
